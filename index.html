What it is

A passkey is a sign-in key your device creates for a specific site or app.

You unlock it with Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint, or device PIN.

2

Why it matters

  • ✓Stops fake login pages (phishing)
  • ✓Faster than passwords and codes
  • ✓No reusable secret to steal
3

Where you'll see it

EmailBankingShoppingSocialWork appsDeveloper tools

Support varies by site and device, but adoption is growing.

How Do Passkeys Compare?

There are lots of ways to secure your accounts. Here's how passkeys stack up against other methods you might be familiar with.

Quick answer: Passkeys are one of the most secure and easiest to use. They can't be tricked by fake websites, and you don't have to remember anything or wait for text messages.

Password

Security LevelVery Weak (AAL1)
Vulnerable to phishing
Not device-bound

Possession

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? We've got answers in plain English.

Not at all! If your phone or computer has a fingerprint reader, face recognition (like Face ID or Windows Hello), or even just a PIN code, you can use passkeys. Most modern devices already have everything you need.
